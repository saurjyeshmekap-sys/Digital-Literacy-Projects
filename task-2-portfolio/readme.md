              Task 2: Building Your Student Digital Portfolio
In today’s world, a professional digital presence isn't just a bonus—it’s expected by employers and the academic community. For this task, I’m stepping into the role of a Student Digital Ambassador by setting up the foundational profiles that will represent me over the next four years.  
🛠️ The Setup Process
Instead of just clicking "Sign Up," I’m curate a professional identity across the platforms that actually matter in tech:  
Platform Selection: I am creating or updating accounts on at least three major professional platforms, such as GitHub, LinkedIn, Kaggle, Stack Overflow, or ResearchGate.  
The GitHub "Landing Page": I’m building a special Profile README (a repository named after my username) that acts as my digital intro, featuring my name, branch, and a quick note on what I’m excited to learn.  
LinkedIn Professionalism: On LinkedIn, I’m making sure my education section is current, including VIT Bhopal, my B.Tech degree, and my expected graduation year.  
Keeping it Minimal: Following the project guidelines, I’m keeping these profiles professional and minimal—no personal photos or private contact details are required at this stage.  
📂 Proof of Work
To prove my portfolio is live, I’m taking clear screenshots of each profile and organizing them into the task-2-portfolio/ folder in my repository.  
📝 Reflections for the Report
In the final project report, I’ll be writing a 150–200 word piece explaining why I chose these specific platforms. I’ll also map out how I plan to use these accounts to showcase my projects and connect with the tech community throughout my degree.
