About This Repository
This repository is my submission for the CSE0001 Digital Literacy project at VIT Bhopal
University. The project is structured around a scenario where I play the role of a Student Digital Ambassador — someone who guides batchmates on navigating the digital world safely and professionally. Each task in this project maps to one of the five course modules, and every piece of work is documented with screenshots, write-ups, and supporting files.
All tools used throughout this project are free and browser-based. No prior coding experience was required, and this project helped me get hands-on with platforms and practices that will actually be useful during my engineering programme.

Module Summaries
Task 1 — Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic (Module 1)
For this task, I designed a one-page infographic using Canva that explains digital literacy to first-year students in a simple, visual way. The design covers what digital literacy means, useful digital tools for students, and safe internet practices. I kept the layout clean so that someone with no prior exposure to the topic could read through it in under a minute and still take something useful away.
The exported infographic is saved inside the task-1-presentation/ folder.
Task 2 — Student Digital Portfolio (Module 2)
In this task, I set up my professional digital presence across three platforms: GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. On GitHub, I created a profile README in a repository named after my username, which includes my name, branch, year, and what I'm hoping to learn during my B.Tech. On LinkedIn, I filled in the Education section with my current degree details and expected graduation year. On Kaggle, I created a student account that I plan to use for data science practice as my coursework progresses.
Screenshots of all three profiles are in the task-2-portfolio/ folder.
Task 3 — Coding and Collaboration Platforms (Module 3)
This task had two parts. For Part A, I created an account on HackerRank and completed a beginner-level Python challenge. It was straightforward, but it helped me understand how these platforms work and why they matter for placements and internships. For Part B, I built a five-question Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz using Google Forms — the kind of quiz I could actually use in my Digital Ambassador role. The form includes both multiple choice and short answer questions.
The Google Form link is shared below. Screenshots of the form and the linked response sheet are in task-3-platforms/.

Task 4 — Professional Email and Etiquette Guide (Module 4)
For Part A, I drafted two professional emails — one from a student to a professor requesting a
deadline extension, and another expressing interest in a summer internship at a company. Both follow proper email structure: a clear subject line, professional greeting, well-organised body, and a polite sign-off. For Part B, I put together a Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist with five points on each side, written specifically for college students.
Both email drafts and the checklist file (social-media-checklist.md) are saved in task-4-email-etiquette/.
Task 5 — Cybercrime Awareness Case Study (Module 5)
For this task, I wrote a 200–250 word case study on phishing — one of the most common cybercrime types targeting students and young adults. The write-up explains what phishing is, how it typically unfolds step by step, who gets targeted, and what the consequences look like. I also created a Stay Safe Online checklist with eight actionable tips for college students in India, including two tips specifically about UPI and online payment safety.
The checklist includes the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and the Helpline number 1930 for reporting incidents.
Both files are saved inside task-5-cybercrime/.
