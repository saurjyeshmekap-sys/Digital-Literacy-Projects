                   Task 4: Drafting a Professional Email & Etiquette Guide
As a Student Digital Ambassador, I know that how we speak online is just as important as the code we write. This task is all about moving past "text speak" and mastering the art of professional communication. I’m creating real-world templates that my batchmates can use when they need to talk to faculty or industry experts.  
🛠️ The Writing Process
I’m breaking this down into two distinct parts—one for formal outreach and one for social media responsibility:
Part A: The Professional Outreach: I am drafting two high-stakes emails that every engineering student will eventually need to send.  
Email 1: A polite, well-structured request to a professor for an assignment extension.  
Email 2: A punchy, professional "cold email" to an internship coordinator expressing my interest in a summer role.  
The Gold Standard: I'm making sure both have clear subject lines, professional greetings, and a proper sign-off. I'll be saving these as a PDF or Word doc in my task-4-email-etiquette/ folder.  
Part B: The Social Media Playbook: I’m also putting together a "Social Media Do’s and Don’ts" checklist.  
The Rules: I’m listing at least 5 things we should definitely do (like networking on LinkedIn) and 5 things we should avoid (like oversharing private info) as college students.  
File Management: This checklist will live in its own file—social-media-checklist.md—inside my task folder.  
📂 Repository Integration
I’m focusing on quality over length here, because a short, well-structured email always beats a long, rambling one. Everything is being organized so a peer could look at my folder and immediately understand how to fix their own online tone.
