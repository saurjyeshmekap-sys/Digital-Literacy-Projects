✅ Responsible Social Media Use – College Student Checklist



✔️ Do’s


Think before you post:
Ask yourself:Consider the statement  “Would I be okay if a professor, recruiter, or my family saw this?” Once something is online, it’s hard to take back.


Keep your profile relatively professional:It is the case that  You don’t need to be overly formal, but having a clean bio, decent profile picture, and 
                                        respectful content helps build a good impression.
                                        
Usage of social media for learning and growth: we should Follow pages related to your field, internships, coding, finance, or
                                         whatever you’re interested in—it can actually help your career.
                                         
Respecting others’ opinions and boundaries:Disagreements are normal, but we should  avoid unnecessary arguments, hate comments, or trolling. 
                                        Stay mature in discussions.
                                        
Manage your time wisely:Ahh. remember the proverb  "Hoist by your own petard" , meaning you are the only who can think good and bad about yourself .It’s easy to lose hours scrolling. Resulting in a loss of precious time.

                        Set limits so social media doesn’t affect your studies, sleep, or productivity.

                        
 ❌ Don’ts
 
Don’t share personal or sensitive information :Avoid posting your phone number, address, passwords, or anything that could risk your privacy or safety.
Don’t post in anger or frustration: It"s like "Marry in haste , repent in leisure"
Emotional posts (especially rants or fights) can damage your image. It’s better to cool down before sharing anything.

Don’t believe or spread everything you see: One must understand "If you don't take time to wweigh the merits , you will get swept away by the lies"
Fake news spreads fast. Always verify information before sharing—it reflects your awareness and responsibility.

Don’t compare your life with others constantly: We all might have heard "The grass is always greener on the other side"
Social media often shows only the “highlight reel.” Comparing yourself can affect your confidence and mental health.

Don’t engage in cyberbullying or negative behavior: We should always follow the glorious lines "Measure twice , cut once" while interacting with someone or somebody over social media.
Even jokes can hurt. Be mindful of your words—online behavior matters just as much as offline.
