

✅ Stay Safe Online – Student Checklist 
1. Think Before You Click
Avoid clicking on unknown links sent via email, WhatsApp, or Instagram DMs—especially those promising prizes,
 internships, or urgent account alerts. Scammers often create a sense of panic to trick you.

3. Use Strong & Unique Passwords
Don’t reuse the same password across apps. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols, and consider a password manager if you have multiple accounts.

4. Turn On Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Enable 2FA on important accounts like email, banking apps, and social media. It adds an extra layer of protection even if your password gets leaked.

5. Beware of Fake Internship & Job Offers
College students are common targets. Always verify companies through official websites or LinkedIn before sharing personal details
or paying any “registration fees.”

7. Protect Your Personal Information
Avoid oversharing details like phone number, college ID, or address publicly on social media. Even small information can be misused.

8. UPI Safety Tip #1 – Never Approve Unknown Requests
If you receive a “collect request” on apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm, do not approve it unless you know the sender.
 Remember: receiving money does NOT require entering your PIN.

10. UPI Safety Tip #2 – Don’t Share OTP or UPI PIN
No bank or payment app will ever ask for your OTP or UPI PIN. If someone does—even claiming to be customer support—it’s a scam.

11. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Transactions
Never log into banking apps or make payments over public Wi-Fi in cafés or hostels. These networks can be unsafe and expose your data.

12. Keep Your Apps & Devices Updated
Regular updates fix security vulnerabilities. Don’t ignore update notifications for your phone, apps, or antivirus software.

13. Log Out from Shared Devices
If you use a friend’s laptop or a college system, always log out from your accounts before leaving.
🚨 If Something Goes Wrong – Report Immediately
Report cyber fraud at: National Cyber Crime Portal (https://cybercrime.gov.in
)
Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (especially for financial fraud—act fast!)
