 Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention Guide
As a Student Digital Ambassador, I know that staying safe online is just as important as being productive. For this final task, I’m stepping into the role of a security researcher to help my peers recognize and dodge the kind of digital traps that specifically target college students.  
🛠️ The Research Process
I’m splitting this task into two parts—one for understanding how these crimes work and one for actually stopping them:
Part A: The Case Study: I am deep-diving into a specific type of cybercrime, like Phishing or UPI/Online Payment Fraud.  
The Deep Dive: I’m writing a 200–250 word breakdown explaining exactly how the scam works, who it targets, and the real-world fallout.  
Real or Realistic: I have the choice to use a real news story or write a realistic "what-if" scenario to make the point clear.  
File Management: I’ll be saving this as casestudy.md or .pdf in my task-5-cybercrime/ folder.  
Part B: The "Stay Safe" Checklist: I’m also putting together a practical survival guide for my batchmates.  
Actionable Tips: I’m listing at least 8 specific tips, with at least two focused entirely on financial and UPI safety since that's a huge risk for us in India.  
The Lifeline: I’m making sure the official reporting channels—the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and the 1930 Helpline—are front and center.  
Organization: This checklist will live as prevention-checklist.md in the task folder.  
📂 Repository Integration
This task isn't just about theory; it’s about building a resource that could actually save a friend from losing money or data. By the time I'm done, my repository will have a dedicated section that acts as a quick-reference guide for digital safety.
