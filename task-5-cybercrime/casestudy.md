 Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
 
 Digital Crimes or Cyber Crimes include illicit acts where computers , networks , or digital devices are used to commit or facilitate criminal activity.
 With flaws like weak international laws , human error , crime-as-a-service these are increasing rapidly.

UPI fraud has become one of the most common cybercrimes among college students and everyday smartphone users.UPI frauds involve phishing, fake customer care fraud,
QR code fraud, and SIM swap attacks.

A well-known real-life example happened in India during the early growth of digital payments, where several people reported losing money 
through fake QR code scams on platforms like OLX. In one such case, a college student listed a second-hand laptop for sale and was contacted by a “buyer” who
seemed genuine and in a hurry to make the payment.

Step by step, the fraud happened like this: first, the fraudster built trust by speaking politely and agreeing instantly to the price without bargaining. 
Then, they created urgency by saying they needed to complete the deal quickly. After that, they sent a QR code, claiming that scanning    allow the student to receive 
the payment. The student, unaware of how UPI actually works, scanned the code and entered their UPI PIN. Within seconds, money was deducted from 
their account instead of being credited.

Such scams mainly target students, elderly individuals, and people who are new to online payments. Fraudsters take advantage of confusion between
“sending” and “receiving” money in UPI systems.

The consequences go beyond just financial loss. Victims often feel stressed, embarrassed, and lose trust in digital platforms. In some cases, 
the lost amount can be significant for a student.

This example clearly highlights the importance of awareness. A simple rule can prevent such fraud: you never need to enter your UPI PIN to 
receive money—only to send it.
Always verify UPI IDs, QR codes, or payment requests before proceeding
